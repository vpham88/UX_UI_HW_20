Homeless Animal Rescue Team (HART)

HART is a no-kill animal rescue and support group that takes in and re-homes abused and unwanted cats and dogs. The website was not user friendly for new pet adopters due to it's lack of transparency with their animals. It also had many hidden pages in the sitemap and needed a restructure.

Problem Statement

People looking to adopt pets want to learn and know more about the pets prior to adoption. This is because they have experienced incompatibility with the pets once they were brought home.

Possible Solution

Greater transparency during the pet adoption process for new pet owners on pet personality and energy. The ability to meet with pets virtually prior to adoption can help to provide more insight into the pet's behavior.

DESIGN PROCESS

EMPATHIZE

Design Plan

In this research project, I helped plan the research and user interview. We conducted a total of 9 user interviews with potential pet adopters in the Washington D.C. area.

  • Many of the users expressed that they had issues of not knowing what their pet's behavior was like prior to adoption
  • Some were afraid to adopt because they heard stories of pet behavior incompatibilities
  • One user had a particularly bad experience with their dog having behavioral issues that required external assistance such as doggy training camp to correct behavioral issues

Our Goals

  • Help users see all of the animals that need homes
  • Help users find animals that need a foster home
  • Help users find their pet match through personality and behavior commonalities
  • Help special pets that have medical conditions and need a special home
  • Provide a way for users to donate to a nonprofit
  • Provide valuable information for pet owners

In order to achieve our goals, we needed to answer the following questions

  • What is the typical process people go through to find pets?
  • What features would help people find their pet match?
  • How do people find their pet match?
  • What is the motivation level of our audience to foster or adopt?

Competitor Analysis

We completed a Competitor Analysis to assess the strengths and weaknesses of current and potential competitors that have similar offerings. We reviewed three direct and three indirect competitors and documented what features each site had.

User Interview

We used the following questions as a base for each interview to determine people's preference on adoption, fostering, and donating to a pet's wellbeing.

  • Age, gender, and relationship status.
  • What social media platforms are you currently active on? (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, etc.)
  • Have you ever adopted a pet through a social media platform?
  • What do you do for a living?
  • What are your interests (extracurricular activities, hobbies, etc.)?
  • Do you have any children?
  • Do you have a history of pets in your home?
  • How did you find your most recent pet and what was your experience?
  • How did you find out about them?
  • How long did it take to find your ideal pet?
  • Are you interested in supporting animal rescue organizations? Do you have any experience in supporting one already, and if so how?
  • What is your first instinct when you see a stray animal?
  • Given the choice of the following options, which action are you most likely to take? Why?
